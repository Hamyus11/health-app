Stay Clean, Stay Healthy!

Keeping our bodies clean helps us fight germs and feel great.

What is Hygiene?

Hygiene refers to conditions and practices that help to maintain health and prevent the spread of diseases. Keeping clean is key to staying healthy.

Types of Hygiene

  1. Personal Hygiene: Caring for one's own body, including bathing, washing hands, and trimming nails.
  2. Oral Hygiene: Practices to keep the mouth, teeth, and gums clean and healthy.
  3. Hand Hygiene: Washing hands with soap and water to remove germs and prevent the spread of illness.
  4. Environmental Hygiene: Keeping our surroundings, like our room and school, clean and organized.
  5. Sleep Hygiene: Habits that help you have a good night's sleep for better health and focus.

Benefits of Good Hygiene

  • Disease Prevention: Good hygiene kills germs and stops the spread of illnesses like the flu.
  • Better Oral Health: Proper dental care prevents cavities, gum disease, and bad breath.
  • Social Confidence: Being clean and fresh helps you feel confident in social situations.
  • Pain Prevention: Preventing infections and dental problems avoids pain and discomfort.
  • Overall Well-being: Cleanliness contributes to a feeling of well-being and self-respect.
